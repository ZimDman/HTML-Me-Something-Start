Daphnes I have worked with

Working as a horticulturist, i have had the pleasure of experiencing many variety of plant material. Few have captured my attention and imagination as the genus Daphne. Through my work in three distinct botanical gardens i have come to find my favorite Daphnes which i hope to share and inspire others to include into your own garden.

All about Daphne

The Daphne genus comes from the family Thymelaeaceae. Mostly consisting of small trees and shrubs, Daphnes are not native to the united states. Depending on whom you talk to and what resource you reference there are between 70 - 95 species. Most of these come from Asia, but some also come from Africa and Europe.

My favorite Daphnes

  1. Daphne odora
  2. Daphne genkwa
  3. Daphne cneorum
  4. Daphne pontica

Daphne odora

Daphne odora is my personal favorite that i got a chance to work with. Imagine walking through a garden in the SouthEastern corner of North Carolina in the middle of January only to smell a fantastic "Bubblegum Orchid" scent! This is a tiny everygreen shrub. The tiny pink flowers themselves are insignificant in display, but pack a powerful odor....hence the name Daphne odora!

Daphne genkwa

Daphne genkwa comes to us from Asia and was used medicinally in China. I have personally been growing this Daphne for 3 years right here in St Louis! They said it couldn't be done. This small shrub gets its common name "lilac daphne" from the color of its tiny flowers. This plant was brought into the Eruopean garden during the age of discovery through a victorian era plant collector.

Daphne cneorum

Daphne cneorum is native to mountain regions of Southern Europe. Small shrub that could be considered for ground cover. This plant is stunning in a rock garden! Fragrant small flowers that are have an attractive aesthetic.

Daphne pontica

Daphne pontica comes to us from the Bulgeria and northern Turkey area. This common name of "Twin flower daphne"is one of the few Daphnes with yellow flowers. This Daphne is known for its leathery like textured leaves.
